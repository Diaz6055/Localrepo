Common heating problems we fix every winter

  • Furnace blows cold air — usually a failed ignitor, flame sensor, or pilot.
  • Heater won't turn on — often a tripped limit switch, blown fuse, or bad thermostat.
  • Heat pump stuck in cooling mode — typically a reversing valve or defrost board issue.
  • Loud bangs at startup — delayed ignition, often from a dirty burner or weak ignitor.
  • Furnace short-cycling — overheating safety control tripping; needs immediate diagnosis.
  • Carbon monoxide detector going off — stop using the system and call us immediately.
  • Strange smells when you turn it on — burning dust is normal the first time each season; persistent smells aren't.
  • Pilot light won't stay lit — usually a thermocouple or gas valve issue.

Should I repair or replace my heater?

If your furnace is under 12 years old and the repair is under $500, repair almost always makes sense. If it's 15+ years old, has had multiple repairs in the last two seasons, or the quote is over $1,000, we'll lay out the math on a replacement so you can make an informed decision. We don't push replacements that don't financially make sense.
