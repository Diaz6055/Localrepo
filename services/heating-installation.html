Heat pumps are the smartest choice for Phoenix homes

Arizona's mild winters are tailor-made for heat pump technology. Unlike a furnace that burns gas to make heat, a heat pump simply moves heat — making it 2 to 4 times more efficient. The same unit cools your home in summer and heats it in winter, delivering year-round comfort from a single piece of equipment. And thanks to the federal Inflation Reduction Act, qualifying heat pump installations now come with a tax credit of up to $2,000.

That said, not every Phoenix home is right for a heat pump. Older homes with poor insulation, no existing ductwork, or natural gas service already in place may benefit more from a high-efficiency furnace or a hybrid dual-fuel system. We'll walk through every option with the actual energy math — not a sales pitch.

Our heating system installation services

Heat pump installation

Single-stage, two-stage, and variable-speed inverter heat pumps from Trane, Carrier, and American Standard. Highest-efficiency models qualify for the $2,000 federal tax credit, plus utility rebates from APS and SRP that can stack to another $1,000.

Gas furnace installation

80% to 96% AFUE single-stage and modulating gas furnaces. Ideal for homes with existing gas service and high heat-load demands. Whisper-quiet variable-speed models reduce winter energy bills by up to 30%.

Dual-fuel hybrid systems

The best of both worlds: a heat pump for mild Phoenix winter days, with a gas furnace backup for the rare night under 30°F. Smart thermostats automatically choose the cheapest energy source minute-by-minute.

Ductless heat pump (mini-split) installation

Perfect for casitas, converted garages, additions, ADUs, and homes without existing ductwork. Whisper-quiet, room-by-room temperature control, and ridiculously efficient.

Replacement gas furnace installations typically run $4,500 to $9,500. Full heat pump systems range from $7,500 to $18,000 depending on tonnage, efficiency rating, and brand.
